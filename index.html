About Diagnostics

Understanding Network Quality

Key Diagnostics Measured

Latency (Round Trip Time)

The time (in milliseconds) it takes for a data packet to travel from your browser to the server and back. Lower latency yields a snappier web experience and is vital for interactive real-time applications like cloud gaming and VoIP calls.

  • < 30 ms: Excellent. Responsive for competitive multiplayer.
  • 30 - 80 ms: Good. Very usable for gaming & video calls.
  • 80 - 150 ms: Fair. Mild delay, fine for streaming/browsing.
  • > 150 ms: Poor. Noticeable lag, sluggish interactions.

Jitter (Latency Consistency)

The variation in latency measurements over time. High jitter causes packet arrival ordering issues, leading to stuttering during video calls, audio buffering, or gaming rubber-banding even if your average ping is acceptable.

  • < 5 ms: Exceptional consistency.
  • 5 - 15 ms: Normal. Safe for streaming.
  • > 15 ms: High Jitter. Potential degradation in real-time streams.

Packet Loss

The percentage of sent data packets that fail to reach the destination. A high packet loss forces network stacks to retry sending data, crippling connections. Packet loss above 1-2% is highly disruptive for interactive media.

WebSockets vs HTTP

WebSockets maintain an active, full-duplex communication channel over a single TCP connection. HTTP sends discrete requests. NetPulse checks WebSocket ping times to test the responsiveness of modern chat, trading, and collaboration tools.
